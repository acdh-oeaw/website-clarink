The point of departure for the project is the fact that up-to-date, empirical, and sociolinguistically grounded quantitative data on multilingualism in Austria have been lacking. Existing information is fragmentary, historically charged and from a sociolinguistic perspective problematic. Moreover, it does not account for inner multilingualism, i.e., the ability of speakers to switch between several varieties or registers of a language. The project *Aspects of (multi-)lingualism in Austria* addresses that research gap by conducting a (socio-)linguistically oriented survey representative for the Austrian electorate population.

For further details, visit the [project website](https://www.oeaw.ac.at/acdh/research/linguistics/research/language-contact/aspects-of-multi-lingualism-in-austria).
